Anova (Analysis of variance) is the most widely analysis tool in statistics that splits a variability in two parts, the systematic part and the random part.

It is used to compare the means of three of more independent treatments and it is an extension of t-test to multiple groups. The key idea is to split the variability in multiple groups and figure out if the differences in the different variability is associated to the groups or just the product of randomness.
